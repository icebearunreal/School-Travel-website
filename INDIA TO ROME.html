How to Reach Rome from India

Your travel guide to the Eternal City

Flight Options

To reach Rome from India, the most convenient way is by air. Here are some tips:

  • Major Airports in India: Delhi (DEL), Mumbai (BOM), Bengaluru (BLR), Chennai (MAA), and Hyderabad (HYD).
  • Destination Airport: Rome–Fiumicino International Airport (FCO), also known as Leonardo da Vinci Airport.
  • Direct Flights: Air India occasionally operates direct flights from Delhi to Rome. Check for availability.
  • Connecting Flights: Airlines like Emirates, Qatar Airways, Lufthansa, Turkish Airlines, and Etihad offer connecting flights via their hubs.
  • Typical flight duration: 9 to 12 hours depending on the route and stopovers.

Visa Information

Before traveling, make sure you have a valid Schengen visa for Italy:

  • Apply through the official Italian consulate or authorized visa application centers in India.
  • Required documents typically include a valid passport, travel itinerary, proof of accommodation, travel insurance, and financial proof.
  • Processing time can take up to 15 days, so apply well in advance.

Check the official Italian Embassy in India website for the latest details.

Travel Tips

  • Book flights early to get better prices, especially during peak tourist seasons (spring and summer).
  • Consider travel insurance that covers health, trip cancellations, and baggage.
  • Learning a few basic Italian phrases can help with local communication.
  • Plan airport transfers from Fiumicino Airport using trains (Leonardo Express), taxis, or shuttle services.
